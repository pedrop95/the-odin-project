Cuban Ropa Vieja

Cuban Ropa Vieja, a cherished and flavorful dish, is a quintessential representation of the island nation's culinary artistry. This delectable creation features tender, slow-cooked shredded beef infused with a rich symphony of flavors, emanating from aromatic spices, bell peppers, onions, and tomatoes. Its name, meaning "old clothes" in Spanish, whimsically captures the dish's appearance, resembling tattered fabric. The savory meat is accompanied by the vibrant medley of vegetables and often served atop a bed of fluffy white rice, resulting in a harmonious marriage of textures and tastes that encapsulate the essence of Cuban cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 pounds beef flank steak
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 (8 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and sliced into strips
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add flank steak and cook until browned, about 4 minutes per side.
  2. Transfer steak to a slow cooker; pour in beef broth and tomato sauce. Add tomato paste, bell pepper, onion, garlic, cumin, cilantro, olive oil, and vinegar; stir until well blended.
  3. Cover and cook on Low for up to 10 hours, or on High for 4 hours.
  4. Shred steak in the slow cooker with two forks.
